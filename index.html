Innovation
The Croker Bolt

Tractrix-Curve Geometry. Zero Compromise.

Traditional oar blades lose up to 10% of applied energy through blade slip. The Bolt's permanently moulded hydrodynamic edge, engineered using Tractrix-curve geometry backed by University of Porto research, captures energy that other blades waste.

Coastal Rowing
Built for Real Conditions

When the water fights back, reach for the pink sleeve.

Coastal rowing is the fastest-growing discipline in the sport — and it's now Olympic. While other oars were designed for protected flatwater courses, Croker blades deliver stability and clean catch in rough water, tidal conditions, and open ocean.

"Concept2 was designed for protected Olympic courses. We were designed for the ocean."

The Croker Slick on S3 shaft: solid but forgiving catch, strong mid-drive loading, clean entry/exit, nimble in all conditions. The S3's 50/50 carbon/glass construction and UV-resistant white epoxy coating are purpose-built for beach-launched boats.
